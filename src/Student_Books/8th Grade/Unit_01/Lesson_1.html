3. Key Grammar: Simple Present and Frequency Adverbs

Learn how to express how often you do things.

üìö

Frequency Adverbs: Position is Key!

We use adverbs like always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never to show frequency.

Rule: Adverb goes before the main verb, but after the verb 'to be'.

PositionExample (Main Verb)Example (Verb TO BE)
Subject + Adverb + VerbI always drink coffee.He is never late.
Subject + Verb + AdverbShe works sometimes (less common).

3.1. Place the Adverb Correctly (5 Items)

Rewrite the sentence placing the adverb in the correct position.

  1. I take a bus to school (usually).
  2. She is tired on Mondays (never).
  3. We eat dinner at 8 p.m. (often).
  4. They watch TV in the morning (rarely).
  5. My brother does the dishes (sometimes).

4. Reading Skill: "A University Student's Day"

Read the text about Sofia's busy routine.

Sofia is a university student. She always wakes up early, around 6:30 a.m. She usually has a quick breakfast. She never takes the bus; she always walks to campus. Her classes start at 8:00 a.m. and finish at 2:00 p.m. In the afternoon, she often studies in the library and sometimes goes to the gym. She rarely goes to bed late. On weekends, she sometimes goes grocery shopping and never does homework on Sundays.

7. Speaking and Self-Assessment

Practice your fluency and check your progress.

7.1. Roleplay: How Often?

Ask a partner (or record yourself) 10 questions using "How often do you...?" and answer using frequency adverbs (e.g., "How often do you cook? I usually cook twice a week.").

Sample Dialogue:
Student A: How often do you go to the gym?
Student B: I usually go three times a week. What about you? How often do you have breakfast?
Student A: I always have breakfast. I never skip it!

Practice Questions (5+):

  1. How often do you check social media?
  2. How often do you study English after class?
  3. How often do you eat fast food?
  4. How often do you exercise?
  5. How often do you call your parents?
  6. How often do you read a book?
