3. Grammar: Present progressive affirmative and negative

We use the Present Progressive to talk about actions happening right now.

üìö

Structure: To Be (am/is/are) + Verb + -ing

Affirmative: Subject + TO BE + Verb-ing + Complement

Negative: Subject + TO BE + **NOT** + Verb-ing + Complement

SubjectTo Be-ing FormExample (Affirmative)Example (Negative)
IamworkingI **am working** right now.I **am not working**.
He/She/ItisstudyingShe **is studying** English.He **isn't reading**.
You/We/TheyarecleaningThey **are cleaning**.We **aren't cleaning**.

3.1. Complete the Sentences (5 Items)

Fill in the blanks using the Present Progressive (Affirmative or Negative).

  1. My mother (cook) dinner right now. is cooking
  2. We (not / watch) TV; we are doing homework. are not watching
  3. The children (play) in the park this afternoon. are playing
  4. I (not / listen) to music; I'm reading. am not listening
  5. My cousin (wait) for the train at the station. is waiting

7. Speaking and Self-Assessment

Practice fluency and check your progress.

7.1. Speaking Practice: Family Activities

In pairs, describe what your family members are doing right now (or what they usually do on this day). Use vocabulary from the lesson and the Present Progressive.

Sample Dialogue:
Student A: What is your sister doing right now?
Student B: She **is studying** for an exam in her room. My father **is not working**; he **is watching** the news.

Practice Questions (5+):

  1. What is your mother/father doing right now?
  2. What are your friends doing this week?
  3. What job is your uncle/aunt doing?
  4. Is your cousin helping with the chores today?
  5. What are the people outside the window doing?
  6. What are you **not** studying in this moment?
